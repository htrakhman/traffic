## Competitor profiles

### Roadsafe Traffic Systems
*Last checked: 2026-04-23*

- **Scale & positioning:** Nation's largest traffic control provider. 50+ branch offices, 1,600+ employees, all 48 continental states. PE-backed (Investcorp + Trilantic North America acquired from ORIX Capital Partners). This is the Walmart of TTC — they win on coverage and institutional contracts, not price or service flexibility. We cannot out-scale them; we win on speed, local relationships, and online discoverability.
- **NJ presence:** Confirmed NJ page at roadsafetraffic.com/new-jersey/. Covers Newark, Jersey City, Camden, Trenton, Atlantic City, Hammonton, Vineland, Bridgeton, Ocean City. Services: roadway striping, traffic control, lane closures, flagging, signage, equipment for purchase or rental. Phone: (888) 650-ROAD. No specific Central NJ focus visible — mostly urban corridors and shore.
- **Pricing:** Quote-only, no public rate card. Same posture as us. They likely have minimum-order thresholds that price out small contractors — our opportunity.
- **Content / SEO:** Company news archive exists but sparse — no recent 2026 blog posts found. Zero informational content targeting "how many cones do I need"-type queries. Their content gap is our AEO wedge.
- **Recent moves:** New CFO appointed (Joe Scarano, 25+ yrs finance experience). 18 total acquisitions, most recent: All Star Striping (Sept 2024, pavement marking). Active hiring events (regional March 2026). Acquisition pace signals aggressive growth, not organic digital investment.

**Our edge vs. Roadsafe:** Small contractors don't call an 800 number at a national company for a 2-day rental. They Google, find an answer to their question, and call whoever gave them that answer. That's the content play we're executing.

## Gaps we'll exploit

- Roadsafe publishes no informational content — every "how many cones / what's taper length / barricade types" query is wide open for us to own organically.
- Their NJ footprint skews toward urban corridors (Newark, Camden) and shore — Central NJ (Middlesex, Mercer, Monmouth, Somerset) is underserved by the nationals.
- Quote-only pricing from nationals = friction for small contractors on tight timelines. If we can show pricing signals (even ranges) on our site, we convert the Googler before they pick up the phone.
