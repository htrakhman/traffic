# Response Playbook

Paste incoming Messenger replies to me and I'll help you draft the response.
For common reply patterns, here's a quick-reference so you can handle most
of them without waiting on me.

**Rule #1:** reply fast (within a few hours during business day) but not
instantly — an instant reply reads like a bot.
**Rule #2:** ask one question per reply. Keep the conversation moving
forward one step at a time.
**Rule #3:** when in doubt, offer to hop on a 5-minute call — voice closes
way more than text for this kind of B2B.

---

## Reply type 1: "We own all our stuff" / "We don't rent gear"

> Totally get it — most of the outfits that have been around a while do.
> Quick one: what about when a job needs more lanes blocked off than usual
> or you get hit with a weekend shutdown? Do you just eat the extra gear
> buy, or rent to cover the spike? (Not trying to sell — legit curious how
> you handle it.)

**Goal:** reframe from "I don't need rentals" → "I rent when I need
to flex capacity." Most do. If they truly never rent, move on.

---

## Reply type 2: "Who is this?" / "What company?"

> Good call — I should've led with that. I run {Your Company Name},
> a traffic-control rental setup based in {Your Town}, NJ. We stock
> cones, barricades, Type III barricades, arrow boards, message boards,
> and full MUTCD signage packages. Mostly serve small/mid contractors
> across Central NJ who need extra gear without buying it. Here's the
> Page: {your FB page link}. Totally fine if it's not a fit — just wanted
> to introduce.

**Goal:** answer the question honestly, then check out. Don't pitch.

---

## Reply type 3: "Send me a price list" / "What do you charge?"

> Happy to — pricing depends a lot on duration and quantity. Quickest way
> is if you tell me roughly what a typical setup looks like for you
> (daily cones, how many arrow boards, signage package) and I'll send
> you a real number instead of a generic list. Could also send over our
> general rate sheet if you prefer — which works better?

**Goal:** avoid sending a cold price list they'll compare on paper and
ghost. Get them to describe an actual job → you quote that job.

---

## Reply type 4: "Send info to my email"

> Sure thing — what's the best one to use? And any specific types of gear
> you're looking at (cones/barricades, arrow boards, full TTC package)?
> I'll tailor it to what's actually relevant.

**Goal:** get the email (move out of Messenger) AND qualify what they
actually need before you send anything.

---

## Reply type 5: Hot lead — "Yeah we rent a lot" / "We have a job next week"

> Nice — what's the job look like? Location, rough duration, and what gear
> you'd need and I can check availability + shoot you a quote today.

**Goal:** move fast. Hot leads go cold in hours in construction.

---

## Reply type 6: Hostile / "Stop messaging me"

> No problem — apologies for the reach-out. Won't bother you again.

Mark as "Do not contact" in the sheet. Move on. Do not argue, do not
follow up, do not try a different angle later. Reputation matters in a
tight local market.

---

## Reply type 7: No response after 4–5 days

**Follow-up message (send once, then drop):**

> Hey, just closing the loop — totally fine if it's not a fit, didn't want
> to leave the thread hanging. If you ever want gear on short notice, I'm
> at {your phone}. Good luck with the season.

**Why it works:** the "closing the loop" framing gets replies because
people feel like they owe you a one-word response. And giving your number
+ walking away makes you look confident instead of desperate.

## Patterns I'll look for when you paste me replies

When you paste a reply, I'll classify it into one of the buckets above
(or flag it as unusual), draft a personalized response, and suggest what
to update in the lead sheet. I'll also flag:

- Whether the person is the decision-maker (owner/PM) vs. admin/front-desk
- Urgency signals ("next week," "tomorrow," "right now")
- Qualifying details you should capture (job type, volume, geography)
- Objections worth pushing on vs. letting go

Just paste the whole thread and tell me the lead's company name.
